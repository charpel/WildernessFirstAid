Disease

Types of Disease Transmission

  1. Direct (Bodily Fluids)
  2. Indirect
  3. Airborne
  4. Vectors (Mosquitoes, etc.)
  5. Water and Food borne

Types of Transmission Prevention

  1. Wash your hands
  2. Hand sanitizer
  3. BSI (Body Substance Isolation) - Gloves, Mask, Suit

Patient Assessment

  1. Scene Survey

    • Is it the scene safe? If not, can you make it safe?
    • Assess clues about MOI (Mechanism of injury) / NOI (Nature of Injury)
    • General impression: Assess severity

  2. Primary Assessment (ABCDDEE)

    3. (Find and fix immediate life threats)

    • Airway (Patent) - check for tongue, vomit, blood, or food covering the airway
    • Breathing
    • Circulation
    • No pulse - do CPR
    • Bleeding - Stop and fix
    • Deformity
    • Disability
    • Environment
    • Everyone else

  3. Secondary Assessment

    4. (CRITICAL: every 5 minutes, STABLE: every 10 - 15 minutes)

    Vital Signs (Don’t tell you what’s wrong)

    • Heart Rate
      • 50 [60 - 80] 100
      • Note: Is it bounding? Irregular? Weak? Strong? Normal?

    • Respiratory Rate
      • 10 [12 - 20] 24

    • Skin (Color/ Temperature/ Moisture)
      • Normal: Pink/ Warm/ Moist
      • Color Undertones:
        • Pink: Normal
        • Yellow: Liver
        • Red: CO poison, heatstroke
        • Blue: Hypothermia
      • Temperature:
        • Warm/ Cold/ Hot
      • Moisture:
        • Moist
        • Clammy
        • Sweaty
        • Dry

    • LOC (Level of Consciousness)
      • ↓ Alert and Oriented (to person, place, time)
      • ↓ Verbal
      • ↓ Reacts to pain
      • ↓ Unresponsive

    Patient History (Pt Hx) - AMPLE

    • Allergies
      • Environmental, Food, Animal, Medicine
    • Medication - SHOPS
      • Street drugs
      • Herbal
      • Over the counter
      • Perscription
      • Sexual enhancement
    • Past pertinent Hx
      • Cardiac
      • Respiratory
      • Seizure Disorder
      • Diabetes
      • Strokes
    • Last ins/ outs
      • Ins - last consumed food, drinks, drugs
      • Outs - urination, bowel movements
    • Events prior

    Physical/ Secondary Examination

    • Fill out a SOAP Note
      • Subjective - chief complaints
      • Objective - vitals, AMPLE
      • Assessment - What the problems are
      • Plan

    Rescue Survey

    • Pt Condition
      • Critical → Rapid Evac
    • Group Condition
    • Communication
      • Set up before dark, etc

Trauma

Orthopedic

  • Ligaments - bone to bone
  • Tendon - bone to muscle
  • Synovial Fluid - lube

  • Evaluation:
    • Sprain Fracture (Fx)
    Swelling Swelling, deformity
    General Pain Pain in specific point
    Bruising Soft tissue injury (Open Fx)
    ↓ROM (Range of movement) ↓ROM (Range of movement)
    JOINT Change in CSM (Circulation, sensation, movement)
    JOINTS AND LIMBS

  • Treatment:
    • Sprain Fracture (Fx)
    Rest 1. Check CSMs
    Ice 2. Pull tractions
    Compress 3. Splint
    Elevation 4. Check CSMs

Spinal Injury

  • MOI (Method of Injury)
    • Rapid deceleration
    • Axial loading (ie shallow diving)
    • Blunt force trauma
    • Fall (2x, 3x your height)
    • Tumbling fall

  • Symptoms
    • Paraesthesis (tingling)
    • Paralysis
    • Change in LOC (level of consciousness)
    • Deformity
    • Bruising, discoloration
    • Point tenderness
    • Generally weak
    • Radiating pains (Shooting pains)
    • Shock

  • Treatment
    • Restrict movement
    • Focused spinal assessment
      1. Alert and Oriented x 3 (To time, place and self)
      2. No distracting injury or medication
      3. No sign or symptom (S/S)
      4. No point tenderness
      5. Neck ROM (Range of Movement)

Soft Tissue Injury

(Not bones)

  • Skin
  • Muscle
  • Fat
  • Cartilage
  • Blood
  • Arteries - away from Heart (↑ Pressure)
  • Veins - towards Heart (↓ Pressure)
  • Capillaries ( oxygen to blood cells)

  • Types of Soft Tissue Injuries
    • Laceration (cut)
    • Puncture wound
    • Impaled object
      • Take out if:
        1. Transport issue (can’t move them without it, etc)
        2. Restricts airway
        3. Metal
    • Amputation
    • Avulsion
    • Abrasion
    • Contusion (bruise)

  • Treatment
    1. Control the bleeding
      • Direct pressure
      • Pressure dressing
      • Tourniquet (cuts of blood flow - last resort)
    2. Clean
      • Irrigate (water with pressure, ie. Drom)
      • Soap and water
      • Debride (make it bleed to clean)
    3. Care

  • Infection
      • Localized infection
      • First:
        • Red
        • Painful
        • Swollen
        • Warm
      • Signs that the infection is turning systemic:
        • Pus
        • Streaking (red line from cut to heart)
      Systemic infection
      • Fever
      • Swollen lymph nods
      • Chills, aches
      • Decreased LOC
      • Shock
  • Burns
    • Superficial (sunburns)
      1. Stop the heat (ie water)
    • Partial-thickness
      • Blisters
      • Lots of pain
      • Decrease in hydration
      1. Don’t pop the blister
      2. Most sterile dressing
    • Full-thickness
    • Friction (blister care)

CPR

  1. Collapse
  2. Pulse, breathing (CAB airway)
    • Only do CPR if they are essentially dead
  3. 30:2 x 5 for 2 min
    • 100 - 120 KpM
  4. CAB → if alive, stop

Contraindications (Don’t do CPR):

  • Signs of life
  • Rigar mortis
  • Dependent Livity
  • Decay
  • Injuries incompatible with life
  • DNR
  • Unsafe scene

Stop:

  • Signs of life
  • Someone with a higher level of training arrives
  • Exhausted
  • Unsafe scene

Rescue Breaths

(Pulse but no breathing)

  • 1 breath every 5 seconds, or until they start breathing

    • * If pregnant: No Heimlich just CPR!

AED

(Automated External Defibrillator)

  • Use every time possible with CPR

Shock

An inadequate profusion of blood/ oxygen through the body

Types of Shock:

  1. Cardiogenic (Basically a heart attack)
  2. Neurogenic Shock
    • Anaphylaxis - vasodilation

      • ↓

    • Sepsis - infection
  3. Hypovolemic
    • Blood
    • Water

Signs and symptoms:

  • No thermoregulation
  • HR ↑, RR ↑
  • BP ↓
  • pale/ cool/ clammy (P/C/C)
  • LOC ↓
  • Erratic, confused, anxious, irritable

Treatment:

  • Keep them warm
  • Treat underlying issues
  • Position of comfort
  • Rapid evac

Environmental

  • Methods of Heat Transfer
    • Conduction - direct touch
    • Radiation - airwaves
    • Convection - movement through a medium
    • Evaporation - sweat

  • Retention of Heat
    • Goosebumps
    • Vasoconstriction
    • Posturing (fetal position)

  • Hypothermia (98.6℉, 37℃)

    • Signs and Symptoms:
      • Cyanotic
      • Fumble, mumble, bumble, stumble, tumble, grumble

    • Treatment:
      • Warm and dry - no wet clothes, layering
      • Hypothermia roll - sleeping bags and tarps
      • Hydrate and eat
      • Move CAREFULLY

  • Freezing and Non-Freezing Injury

    • Frostbite

      • (Frozen tissue → cell death)

      • Superficial
        • Red, numb, tingly
      • Partial-thickness
        • Pain when thawed
      • Deep (3rd)
        • Rock hard

    • Trench foot
      • Keep feet dry at night

  • Heat Injuries

    • Hyponeutrimia
      • Too much water

    • Heat Exhaustion

      • Signs and Symptoms
        • Sweaty
        • P/C/C
        • HR↑ RR↑
        • Nausea
      • Treatment
        • Water
        • Rest
        • Shade

    • Heat Stress
      • Same as heat exhaustion but a little worse

    • Heat Stroke
      • Change in LOC
      • ↑105℉
      • Red or bone dry

  • Lightning

    • Signs and Symptoms:
      • Burns
      • Cardiac arrest
